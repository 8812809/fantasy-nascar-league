League Structure

The Fantasy NASCAR League is a 10-team private fantasy league. Each team carries 4 active NASCAR Cup Series drivers/cars during each segment.

  • 10 league teams
  • 4 drivers/cars per team
  • 3 scoring segments during the season
  • 12 races per segment
  • Full redraft after each segment

Team Rosters

Each team is responsible for the 4 drivers/cars drafted to its roster for the current segment. All points earned by those drivers/cars count toward that team's weekly score and segment standings while they are active on that roster.

Driver/car numbers shown on the website are for roster identification only. Scoring is based on the official NASCAR Cup Series race results.

Segment Format

The season is divided into 3 segments of 12 races each. At the end of each segment, the segment standings are finalized and a full redraft is held before the next segment begins.

Segment standings reset after each redraft. Overall season records may continue to be tracked on the website for league history and reference.

Weekly Competition

Each race week, every team's score is calculated by adding the qualifying/start points and race finish points earned by all 4 active drivers/cars on that team.

The team with the highest weekly score wins the weekly side bet for that race.

Segment Champion

The Segment Champion is the team with the most points over the full 12-race segment.

No tiebreaker rules are used for segment championships unless the commissioner announces a correction or clarification.

Scoring System

Qualifying / Starting Position Points

Starting position points are awarded from 1st through 40th:

  • 1st starting position = 40 points
  • 2nd starting position = 39 points
  • 3rd starting position = 38 points
  • Points continue down by 1 point per position
  • 40th starting position = 1 point

Race Finish Points

Race finish points are awarded from 1st through 40th:

  • 1st finishing position = 120 points
  • 2nd finishing position = 117 points
  • 3rd finishing position = 114 points
  • Points continue down by 3 points per position
  • 40th finishing position = 3 points

Weekly Team Score: qualifying/start points plus race finish points for all 4 active drivers/cars on the team.

Driver Transactions

  1. Each team gets one car change per segment.
  2. No team may make a car change until after the first race of the segment is completed.
  3. Any change must be completed before qualifying begins to be included for that race.
  4. All points already earned by the dropped car remain with that team.
  5. The newly added car begins earning points for that team once active.
  6. After a team uses its one transaction, that team is roster locked until the next segment draft.

Roster Status

🔓 Eligible: Team has not used its one transaction for the current segment.

🔒 Locked: Team has used its one transaction and cannot make another change until the next segment draft.

Official Results Source

Scoring is based on official NASCAR Cup Series qualifying/start information and official race results. If results are corrected after the race, the commissioner may update league scoring and standings.

Website and Standings

The website is the official league record for posted rosters, race results, weekly winners, segment standings, and league history.

Corrections may be made if a scoring, roster, data-entry, or website display error is found.

Commissioner Authority

The commissioner has final authority on scoring disputes, roster issues, corrections, transaction timing, and rule interpretations.

General League Conduct

The league is intended to be competitive, fair, and fun. Teams are expected to respect league decisions, follow transaction deadlines, and communicate any roster or scoring concerns as soon as possible.
